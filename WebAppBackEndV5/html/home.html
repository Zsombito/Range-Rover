About Us

The RangeRover is a prototype Mars Rover inspired by NASAâ€™s Perseverance. It was developed as a university project for the 2nd year Electronic and Information/Electrical Engineering course at Imperial College London. Its objective is to serve as a prototype and as a model for the behaviour of a rover surveying alien colonies on Mars. It has many features as will be explained in the introduction section of the full technical report provided. This website has a main page which serves as heads up display (HUD) for the rover. There are statistics on battery health, data from surveying, and controls for the rover. The team consists of 4 EIE students and 3 EEE students as shown below, they are all proud of this project and have worked extremely hard over the course of a month.

How to use

The website has 3 main purposes. The first is to drive the rover maunally or automatically. To enter manual mode just turn off the auto drive switch. Once in manual you can click and hold on the buttons to move and turn the rover for as long as they are held. You can also click a coordinate on the map to make the rover rover move ther, or type the coordinate in manually. We can also see the live position of the rover and obstacles it sees on the map.
